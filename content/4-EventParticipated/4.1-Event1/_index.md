# Summary Report: “AWS EVENT”

### Event Objectives

- Share knowledge and experiences regarding academic and professional journeys.
- Provide tips for personal growth and self-improvement.
- Learn about the BMAD methodology.

### Guest Speakers

- **Mr. Long** - How to be as Addicted to Learning as to Social Media
- **Mr. Thinh** - Automated Prompt Engineering
- **Mr. Khang** - Why Haven't You Started Working Yet?
- **Ms. Thu** - BMAD

### Key Highlights

#### Mr. Long - How to be as Addicted to Learning as to Social Media
- Identified the core reasons behind academic procrastination.
- Suggested a consistent learning approach, such as studying a little bit every single day consecutively.
- Provided real-world examples of building habit streaks.
- Shared tips to overcome procrastination, notably the 2-minute rule.

#### Mr. Thinh - Automated Prompt Engineering
- Discussed the reasons why writing generic or overly broad prompts should be avoided.
- Outlined the negative consequences of uncontrolled and over-reliant AI usage.
- Demonstrated methods to optimize prompts for more accurate and effective results.

#### Mr. Khang - Why Haven't You Started Working Yet?
- Shared practical experiences and insights on transitioning from graduation into the corporate work environment.

#### Ms. Thu - BMAD
- Introduced an overview of the BMAD performance management methodology.
- Provided guidance on how to implement and apply it effectively.



### Key Takeaways

- Gained a clear understanding of procrastination and its damaging effects.
- Learned step-by-step methods to tackle procrastination in any task.
- Discovered how to organize goals and actions systematically instead of working only when inspired.
- Recognized the pitfalls of using prompts aimlessly without a clear intent.
- Mastered prompt optimization techniques to utilize AI in the most efficient and resource-saving way.
- Identified the major differences between the academic environment and the corporate world.
- Understood the core differences between working on a university assignment (course project) and executing a real-world project for a client.
